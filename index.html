A Brief History...


Latham Chambers receives its name from the Right Honourable Sir John Greig Latham (1877-1964) who served as the fifth Chief Justice of the High Court of Australia from October 1935 to April 1952. He had earlier served as Attorney-General of Australia from 1932 to 1934 and Leader of the Opposition from 1929 to 1931. Latham Chambers, originally apart of Chalfont Chambers, moved its base to Level 8 of the Culwulla Chambers Building, located at 67 Castlereagh Street, Sydney. The building was constructed in 1912 at 50m high, becoming the tallest building in Sydney until 1961. Latham Chambers became independent and formally commenced operations in 1998.
